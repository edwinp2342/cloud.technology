Seoul

Seoul's streets are like a living canvas, where old meets new in a colorful urban symphony. From the lively markets of Namdaemun and Dongdaemun to the sizzling street food scene that tempts your taste buds with tteokbokki and hotteok, the city's streets are a lively mix. Myeongdong and Insadong beckon with their shopping delights, while pedestrian-friendly zones and cozy coffee shops invite you to take a leisurely stroll. When the sun sets, neighborhoods like Hongdae and Itaewon light up with vibrant nightlife, and even the subway stations offer an underground world of surprises. The city's street fashion, creative street art, and an array of street festivals keep the excitement alive, making each walk a cultural journey.

Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung Palace, located in the heart of Seoul, South Korea, is a magnificent historical site that exemplifies the grandeur of the Joseon Dynasty. With its striking architecture, ornate gates, and vast courtyards, it stands as a symbol of Korean culture and heritage. Visitors can explore the beautifully preserved buildings, including the iconic Geunjeongjeon Hall, and immerse themselves in the rich history and traditions of Korea.
